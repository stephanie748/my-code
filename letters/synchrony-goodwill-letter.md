# Goodwill letter — Synchrony Bank / Sam's Club Mastercard

Purpose: politely ask Synchrony to remove the late-payment mark from her credit
report as a one-time courtesy, because her autopay lapsed during a move without
her knowing. Fill in the [brackets] and send to the address on her statement
(or call and read it as a script). Keep a copy.

---

[Your Full Name]
[Your Current Mailing Address]
[City, State ZIP]

[Date]

Synchrony Bank — Sam's Club Mastercard
[Mailing address from the back of your statement or your bill]

Re: Goodwill request to remove a late payment — Sam's Club Mastercard, account ending 6066

To Whom It May Concern,

I have been a Sam's Club Mastercard customer since 2022, and I am writing to
respectfully ask for a goodwill adjustment on my account.

I have always valued this account and worked hard to stay in good standing —
including setting up the monthly automatic payment arrangement that I negotiated
with your team. Recently, however, I moved, and during that transition my
automatic payments unexpectedly stopped drafting. I was not aware that the
autopay had failed, and as a result a payment was reported late. This does not
reflect my genuine intention or my long history with this account.

As soon as I discovered the problem, I took steps to bring the account current
and to restart my automatic payments so that this will not happen again.

Given my history as a loyal customer, and the fact that this was an honest
oversight caused by the autopay lapsing during my move, I am kindly asking
whether you would consider removing the late payment from my credit report as a
one-time goodwill gesture. I would be sincerely grateful — it would mean a great
deal to me as I work to keep my credit in good shape.

Thank you very much for your time and for considering my request. Please feel
free to contact me at [your phone number] or [your email] if any further
information would be helpful.

With appreciation,


[Your signature]
[Your Full Name]
Account ending: 6066

---

## Tips for sending it
- **Get the account current first if you can.** They are much more likely to say
  yes when the past-due is already paid.
- **Where to send it:** the mailing address on the back of your statement/bill.
  You can also **call** the number on the back of the card and say the same thing
  out loud — ask for a "goodwill adjustment" to remove the late payment.
- **Never share a password or verification code** with anyone helping you — only
  the bank, and only when you placed the call yourself.
- **Keep a copy** of the letter and note the date you sent it.
- If the first person says no, it's okay to politely thank them and try again
  later or ask for a supervisor — goodwill requests sometimes take a second try.
