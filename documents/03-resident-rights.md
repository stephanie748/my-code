# Resident Rights

**Recovery Residence:** {{HOUSE_NAME}}
**Effective Date:** {{EFFECTIVE_DATE}}

Every resident of {{HOUSE_NAME}} is guaranteed the following rights, consistent with the NARR Standard and PARR Code of Ethics:

1. **Dignity and Respect** — To be treated with dignity and respect regardless of race, color, national origin, religion, sex, disability, or source of income, consistent with fair housing law.
2. **Safe and Sanitary Housing** — To live in a safe, sanitary residence that meets applicable fire, health, and occupancy codes.
3. **Freedom from Abuse** — To be free from physical, verbal, emotional, or financial abuse or exploitation by staff, house management, or other residents.
4. **Privacy** — To reasonable privacy, subject to routine house inspections for safety and rule compliance disclosed in the House Rules.
5. **Grievance Process** — To raise a grievance or complaint without fear of retaliation, using the Grievance Procedure.
6. **Access to Recovery Supports** — To access 12-step meetings, recovery support services, medical care, and case management as needed.
7. **Informed Consent** — To receive, in writing, all fees, rules, and conditions of residency prior to move-in.
8. **Due Process on Discharge** — To receive notice and reason for discharge except in cases of immediate safety risk, per the Relapse & Discharge Policy.
9. **Right to Personal Property** — To retain and secure personal property, subject to prohibited-items restrictions in the House Rules.
10. **Right to Communicate** — To reasonable access to a phone and mail for communication with family, sponsors, and providers.
11. **Non-Retaliation** — To be free from retaliation for filing a grievance, contacting DDAP, or contacting PARR/NARR.
