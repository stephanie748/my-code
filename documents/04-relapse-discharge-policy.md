# Relapse & Discharge Policy

**Recovery Residence:** {{HOUSE_NAME}}
**Effective Date:** {{EFFECTIVE_DATE}}

## 1. Philosophy

Lifeline recognizes relapse as a possible part of the recovery process. This policy balances resident safety, house safety, and the recovery goals of all residents.

## 2. Relapse Response

1. Any resident who tests positive for or is found to have used drugs/alcohol will be immediately removed from the residence for the safety of the house.
2. The house manager, {{HOUSE_MANAGER_NAME}}, will contact the resident's emergency contact and, where applicable, treatment provider or case manager.
3. Resident may be referred to a higher level of care (detox, inpatient) before any consideration of re-admission.
4. Re-admission after relapse is at the discretion of {{HOUSE_MANAGER_NAME}} / Lifeline Medical Group Inc. and is not guaranteed.

## 3. Grounds for Immediate Discharge

- Violence or threats of violence toward staff or residents.
- Possession of weapons, illegal drugs, or drug paraphernalia on the premises.
- Theft from the house, staff, or other residents.
- Bringing alcohol or drugs onto the premises (distinct from personal use/relapse off-site).

## 4. Grounds for Discharge with Notice

- Repeated House Rules violations after warnings.
- Non-payment of rent per the Financial Policy.
- Failure to engage in required program/employment participation.

Notice period for non-emergency discharge: {{DISCHARGE_NOTICE_PERIOD}}.

## 5. Appeal

Residents may appeal a discharge decision (other than immediate safety discharges) through the Grievance Procedure within {{APPEAL_WINDOW}} of notice.

## 6. Transition Support

Upon discharge, staff will provide, where possible, referrals to detox, treatment, shelter, or other recovery housing to avoid discharge into homelessness.
