## Pennsylvania-specific leads (researched July 2026)
- **DDAP (PA Dept. of Drug and Alcohol Programs)** funds recovery housing with opioid settlement money — e.g. $2M+ to organizations improving access to **DDAP-licensed recovery houses**, and $1.2M for young-adult (18–24) recovery housing via Single County Authorities. PA expects ~$1.07B–$2.2B in settlement money through 2038, and recovery housing is an approved use. Key step: get the sober living home **licensed through DDAP** — that's the gate to state referrals and grants. Watch pa.gov/agencies/ddap for grant announcements.
- **County opioid settlement grants** — counties (e.g. Dauphin, Delaware) run their own opioid remediation grant programs. Check her county's program (need to learn her county).
- **MATP (Medical Assistance Transportation Program)** — PA's Medicaid ride program, run county by county. This is separate from the MCO that rejected her, so the transportation door may still be open: her county's MATP office contracts with local transportation providers. County contacts at matp.pa.gov/CountyContact.aspx.
- **PennDOT Section 5310** — accessible-van funding through PennDOT; mostly nonprofits but shared-ride providers can qualify.
- **PA Small Diverse Business certification (via PA DGS)** — free certification as minority- and woman-owned; unlocks state contracting set-asides. Worth doing.

## Best-fit grants found (July 2026)
1. **HerRise MicroGrant** — $1,000 every month for a business at least 51% owned by a woman of color, under $1M revenue. Simple application (two short essay questions), $15 fee. Deadline: last day of each month. hersuitespot.com
2. **Amber Grant (WomensNet)** — $10,000 monthly for women-owned businesses, plus year-end $25K/$50K awards. Rolling monthly deadline, $15 fee. ambergrantsforwomen.com
3. **Galaxy Grants (Hidden Star)** — $3,500 for women- or minority-owned businesses. Free to enter. Deadline was April 30, 2026 — check for the next round.
4. **NAACP programs (e.g. Powershift Entrepreneur Grant)** and **Hello Alice grants** — recurring grants for Black-owned and minority-owned businesses; rounds open and close through the year. helloalice.com
5. **State minority/women business grants** — need her state to look these up.
6. **Federal**: MBDA business centers (free help, not cash), SBA 8(a) certification (helps win government contracts, not a grant), grants.gov for federal grants.

## Sober living funding (researched July 2026)
- Most government money for sober living (SAMHSA, HUD Recovery Housing Program) goes to **nonprofits** and flows through **state agencies** — for-profit homes usually can't get it directly. If she's for-profit, a nonprofit arm may be worth discussing later.
- Big current sources: SAMHSA recovery housing grants ($50K–$500K), State Opioid Response funds ($1.575B for FY2026), and **state opioid settlement money** (varies a lot by state — e.g. Michigan put $37.5M into recovery housing in 2026).
- Funders check three things first: legal entity structure, state certification, and NARR (National Alliance for Recovery Residences) alignment. Getting NARR-certified is a key early step.
- Useful guides: soberlivingapp.com funding guide, HUD Exchange Recovery Housing Program page.

## Transportation funding (researched July 2026)
- **Section 5310 (Enhanced Mobility of Seniors & Individuals with Disabilities)** — federal money via state DOTs that buys accessible vans; 80% federal / 20% local match. Mostly for nonprofits and public agencies, though shared-ride providers can sometimes qualify. Apply through the state DOT.
- **Medicaid NEMT (non-emergency medical transportation)** — not a grant, but steady per-ride payment if she enrolls as a Medicaid transport provider in her state. Could pay for the van over time.
- The small women/minority business grants above (HerRise, Amber, Galaxy, Hello Alice) can be put toward a van — grant money from those is flexible.
