# Switching Medicare insurance from Humana — research notes

*Last updated: July 10, 2026. Saved so this research is never lost between sessions.*

## What she's doing

She currently has Humana medical insurance and wants to switch to whichever
plan is the best fit for a senior. She thought the other company might be
Aetna, but she isn't sure — the real goal is "best fit," not one specific
company.

## Her situation (learned July 10, 2026)

- She used to have a **Humana PPO in Florida** and loved its low copay for
  **physical therapy** — PT is something she uses regularly, so the PT copay
  is one of the most important things to compare in any new plan.
- She is now in the **Philadelphia, PA** area, and coverage there is costing
  her more than Florida did.
- **Important:** moving from Florida to Pennsylvania is a "qualifying life
  event" — it opens a special window to switch plans without waiting for
  fall. The window generally lasts through 2 full months after the month of
  the move. Need to find out when she moved to know if it's still open.

## Philadelphia-area options (2026)

- **Independence Blue Cross** is the dominant local insurer — its
  **Keystone 65** (HMO) plans are the most popular in Philadelphia County,
  and **Personal Choice 65 (PPO)** is its PPO like her old Humana plan.
- **Aetna**, **Humana**, and **UnitedHealthcare** also offer plans there.
- Philadelphia County has about 66 Medicare Advantage plans; 42 have no
  monthly premium beyond the standard Part B premium.
- PT copays vary plan by plan and aren't in news articles — compare on
  medicare.gov's plan finder (it shows the physical therapy copay for each
  plan) or call the insurer.
- Note: Florida plans are famously generous because of heavy competition
  there; PA plans typically cost a bit more. That's normal, not something
  she did wrong.

## The most important thing: timing

Medicare only lets you switch plans during certain windows:

- **October 15 – December 7 (every fall):** anyone can switch. The new plan
  starts January 1. **This is her next opportunity** — as of July 2026, the
  earlier windows for this year have already closed.
- **January 1 – March 31:** people already on a Medicare Advantage plan can
  make one change.
- **Special exceptions any time of year:** if she moves, or if Humana stops
  offering her plan in her county, she can switch right away without waiting.
  Worth checking — Humana is pulling out of many counties in 2026, so if she
  gets a letter from Humana saying her plan is ending, that letter is her
  ticket to switch immediately.

## Well-regarded alternatives to Humana (2026)

- **AARP / UnitedHealthcare** — biggest network of doctors in the country;
  many $0-monthly-premium plans that include dental, vision, and hearing.
  Often named the best overall pick for 2026.
- **Aetna** — high quality ratings from Medicare itself; a solid choice, and
  likely the one she remembered.
- **Kaiser Permanente** — the highest quality/satisfaction ratings, but only
  available in certain states and you must use their own doctors.

## What actually decides "best fit" (personal, not brand)

The right plan depends on her answers to these — collect before comparing:

1. Her ZIP code (plans differ county by county).
2. Which doctors she wants to keep — check they take the new plan.
3. Her regular prescriptions — check they're covered cheaply.
4. Whether she'd rather pay less per month or be protected from big bills.

The official, safe place to compare plans side by side is **medicare.gov**
(the government's own site). Free unbiased human help: **SHIP** (State Health
Insurance Assistance Program), 1-877-839-2675.

## Reminder to future Claude

Never help enter passwords, card numbers, Medicare numbers, or verification
codes. Help her compare and understand; enrollment itself she does with a
trusted person or SHIP counselor.
